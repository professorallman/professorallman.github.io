Happy Easter

        	George never complained about how little he had. He was always a grateful boy for the things that he has in his life. He was happy to still have a mother even though his father had run out on the family long before he could remember. George has four older brothers:  Tim, Mark, and Luke. Tim is a twelve year old, and looks after George more like a father than a brother. Mark is a sixteen year old so wrapped up in his own problems that he does not notice what is going on with the family. The oldest brother, Luke, is eighteen years old. Luke is a couch hopper, but would shows up from time to time to raid the refrigerator. George is the youngest of the bunch only eight years old, and still retained some of the innocence of the world in him. He did not remember their father the way his brothers had. The only things George knew about his father where what his brothers would tell him. They told George about how violent their father had gotten just before leaving the family. George was not harmed since he was only two years old at the time. Although George did not experience his father first hand he is living the consequences of his actions every day. Their mother has become overly protective and does not allow the boys out much. The family has become distant from one another. Meal times consist of over processed food served in sectioned trays. At least they were all together while they ate, even if all of them were glued to the television.

        	George was invited to attend an Easter luncheon by his best friend Joe. George had to beg his mother to allow him to go. George's Mother had a hard time trusting anyone. Being a single mother she felt like the only way to protect her children was to keep them as close as possible. George often wondered if this was why Luke was never around anymore.  After hours of pleading she finally said it was ok for George to go.

        	It is the day before Easter and George is up early getting ready for the luncheon. He shares a room with all his brothers in their small two bedroom apartment. George shuffles through the pile of dirty clothes looking for something that didn't smell like wet dog. He puts on a red and white plaid dress shirt that is missing the bottom button. The back of his shirt had a small hole from jumping over the fence at the playground; Mark had an amazing throwing and overshot the field a little bit. He put on a pair of Tim’s blue jeans which were two sizes too big for him. He runs down to wait in the lobby for Joe to come and pick him up. Thirty minutes roll by before Joe and his mother pull up in their white 1993 Chevrolet Cavalier. George jumps into the back seat and joins his friend Joe.

        	"Happy Easter Joe!"

        	"Happy Easter George! I'm so excited your mom let you come over!"

        	"Me Too!"

        	They sit in the back talking about how much they hate homework and how girls are icky.

They pull up to the house and join joes family inside. It was just joe and his little sister Amanda with their mom and dad tis big house. This was the biggest house George had even been in. The only one he could think of that was bigger was a house his aunt Margie and her friend Betty rented out. They each had four kids in a smaller house then Joes. But onlike Aunt Margies house and Georges apartment everything in Joes house was clean. It looked like everything had a place and a purpose. Joe gave George the grand tour around the house. The living room had an actuall coach instead of folding chairs. In the kitchen the stove had all the burners instead of only one. George could see the dishes stoed away behind glass panel cuppard dors. At Georges most things that were glass would break during a fight with his brother. At georges all the dirty dishes were stacked to the left and the clean in the dish strainer on the right. He knew was the purpose of this was because they were going to get dirty anyway why put them up so high. Every room Joe showed was cleaner then the last. They step into the dining room and join joes family at the table. They bow their heads and say grace. George thought that the food infront of him could feed his family for a year. After dinner they went into the backyard for an easter egg hunt .

"What's an egg hunt?" George asks

"It's were the Easter bunny hides little plastic eggs all around the backyard filled with money and treats"

"Oh, I guess he is just late to visit my house then" George replies.

Everyone finishes up dinner and makes their way to the backyard. All of the children are given baskets and the egg hunt starts. Joe and his sister's basket fill up emmidiatly as if they had already known where their parents had hid all of the eggs. Or maybe it was just years of experience. George was only able to find four eggs. Inside the blue egg was four quarters. In the red egg there is a yellow and red startbust. The last two eggs contain an aassortment of jelly beans.

George did not eat the candy in his eggs. He took them home and hid them around the apartment. The next day George made the table setting out plate's silverware and glasses for everyone. Since it was Easter no one would be having Luke stay over on their couch since they had a day planned with families.  Georges mom fixed t.v dinners for everyone in the oven. :  Tim, Mark, and Luke grabed their lucnes as sat infront of the t.v and began eating. George takes his tv dinner and scoops out the ontents onto a plate. He sits down and says grace eating his lunch alone at the table. Afterword while his brothers are still all out in the living room whatching tv George runs around collecting the plastic eggs. He opens the blue one with the four quarters and says

"Wow nice find Luke! What did you get Tim?"

George opens up the red egg with the two starbursts

"Were going to have to get a nife to split that with everyone! What about you Mark? What did you get?"

George opens up the last egg and acts as though he had not known the contents. He puts the cover back on all of the eggs. He hides the eggs in his sock drawer for next year's egg hunt. George joins his family in the living room and they all watch tv.
