"Good evening the time is now 1:55 a.m. and the Parks library will be closing in five minutes. Just a reminder that the Parks library will be closed tomorrow for the big game against the Huskers. Go Cyclones!"            

On the top shelf was the last copy of "Confluence Results for a Quantum Lambda Calculus with Measurements". Zeke Lawson is on a step stool reaching up for the book with his right hand. Lillian Mathason, right hand full of books, reaches for it with her left hand. Her delicate hand carefully grips His. Their eyes meet; Zeke cannot tell if they're green or if they're blue.

***

A young girl and her mother are out for a walk in their neighborhood in the small town of Hardwick Minnesota. Each house they pass by has character and features separating them from each other. They round the corner to a predominantly elderly block. Each house they pass the residence take one look at the girl and close their curtains or slam their doors shut. The little girl stops in her tracks still holding her mother's hand jerking her back slightly.

 "Mommy why do all the old people stare at me?"

"Oh lily, stop being silly."

They were in front of a single story brick rambler house. Just to the right of the door was a double wide square window looking into the living room. There are fresh looking maroon curtains draping on either side of the window with a swirly gold pattern. In the window was an elderly couple peering out right into the blue eyes of the little girl and into her soul.

"Did you need help with that one Lily?"

"What? Oh, no, I'm fine." Lillian was staring at the faded maroon curtains with peeling gold patterns hanging in the front window of their new house. The newlyweds had just purchased a home in Lillian’s hometown of Hardwick and were getting settled in. Lillian’s father offered a job at the town’s ethanol plant to Zeke. Zeke had a week to get settled into his new house before he had to start at the plant. There were some gardening tools in the kitchen that needed to be moved to the Garage. Lily carried the posthole digger, rake, and broom out to the garage. Zeke followed carrying an assortment of shovels. They placed the tools against the back wall just in front of their old Toyota Camry parked in the garage. Zeke notices a slab of white marble sitting in the other corner. As he approaches he notices that it is broken diagonally across both top and bottom.

       	"Holy crap it's a tombstone!" Zeke said.

       	"How did we miss this?" Lily asked, now joining Zeke next to the tombstone.

The parts of the tombstone that were still visible read:

JAN         	.

DEC 13 20  .

WIFE OF

PFC

JAMES G

CARVER

USA

 

"Carver, anyone you know?"  Zeke asks.

"Never heard of James Carver, I wonder what his wife's name was." Lily replied.

They decided that they would call the caretaker of the town cemetery on Monday to see if any tombstones had gone missing. Lily and Zeke made their way back to the house to continue unpacking. Zeke found the box with his laptop in it.

"Hey, want to try and look up James Carver?"

Before Lily could even respond Zeke was already booting up his laptop. He pulls up Google and enters the name into the search. Just as he clicks on the first result the front door knocks.

"Creepy!" Lily says.

Zeke sets his laptop down and walks over and opens the door. There was an older gentleman standing at the door. He was wearing an old trench coat and blue jeans.

           "Evenin' Sir, sorry to interrupt"

       	"Oh not a problem, what can I do for you?"

       	"Well you see sir; I grew up in this house. Was my parents' house. When my mom passed we tried to get her buried in the military cemetery but they wouldn’t take her. We had already done gone and purchased the tombstone and everything. And you see, it’s like this, I heard from my Sister that our cousins had left it here. Have you seen it?"

       	Zeke thought his story was a bit odd. But he also wondered what the odds of a man showing up for a tombstone that they had found that day.

       	"Oh yea, we found it in the garage. Why don't you swing around back and well meet you there?"

          They walked to the back of the garage where the marble slab had been earlier that day. It was gone!

       	"It was right here earlier, I don't know what happened to it. Do you have a number I can get ahold of you in case it turns up?"

           "Liar!" the man shouts while pulling out a knife. He begins brandishing it in the air drunkenly with no particular target. Lillian lets out a shriek. Zeke steps back and grabs the shovel resting against the back wall. He takes a swing at the man. It connects with the man's face and he falls backwards. He hits the back of his head on the bumper of the Camry and a loud crack is heard. Blood runs out from the back of the man’s head and begins to coat the garage floor.

          Zeke is cradling Lilly, who is drenched in tears, when the ambulance and Sherriff show up. The paramedics declare the man dead and put him in a body bag on a stretcher. The Sherriff fetches the man's wallet out of his pocket.

           "James G Carver the 2nd , a war vet like his father." The Sherriff takes down Zeke & Lily's story in a little black notepad. “Well that should do it, call me if you have any questions”

       	"Hey sheriff" Zeke calls out to the sheriff as he is walking to his squad car “What was his mother's name?”

       	The sheriff stopped in his tracks. He did not turn around and just said "Don't worry about it Zeke." He gets into his squad car and drives off.

          The next day Zeke and Lilly go to the garage to clean up the mess. Lily sobs quietly as she mops up the blood on the floor. The garage door opens and James G Carver the 2nd walks in. Lily screams and faints falling backwards into Zeke.  James walks over and picks up his mother's tombstone and walks out. As he is leaving Zeke notices that his trench coat is now made of wallpaper, the exact pattern that was in the living room. Zeke sets Lily gingerly on the ground and runs outside after him. He was only inches behind him but when Zeke stepped out of the door, James was gone.

Zeke rushes inside to the kitchen and calls an ambulance. As he sets the phone back on the receiver he looks into the living room. He was sure now that the wallpaper was made up of the same pattern that James was wearing.  The ambulance arrives and takes Lily away. As soon as they are out of sight Zeke rushes into the house and begins ripping off the living room wallpaper. As he did blood began dripping from the spots that he pulled at, it was like he was picking a scab. When all the wallpaper was off he stepped back and could see that the blood formed a message on the wall.

He read it aloud "Sins of the past haunt those who run the fastest. And those that come back to forget."

As soon as he finished reading this the room went black; the floor beneath him disappeared into the same blackness. He began free fall through the dark.  He tumbled down until he hit the ground of the very room he had just been in. The room was different though, the walls were freshly painted and everything was antique but new. There was a man sitting in a green recliner staring into a black and white TV.

Zeke started yelling at the man. "What the fuck is going on here?"

But the man just sat there, unmoved. Zeke ran up and tried to grab the man but his hands passed right through him causing a chill to pass through his veins.

The man began to move his mouth, but no sound was coming out. Then all of a sudden he was audible:

"When is dinner going to be done?"

       	Zeke could hear a woman respond from the kitchen "In twenty minutes."

Zeke made his way into the kitchen to see a woman working furiously over the stove. As he approached the woman the room filled with the same coldness he had felt when he fell into the man in the green chair.  Zeke began to tremble and walk more cautiously toward the women. When he was only a foot away the woman turned around. Her face was severely burned. Half of her mouth was held together with stitches. Tears of blood ran down her cheek. Her eyes had also been sewn shut.  Zeke fell backwards. The woman made her way to the sink and deposited some dirty bowls. She was functioning like nothing was wrong with her. She made her way back to the stove and continued to cook. Zeke stood up and ran to the door to the left of the woman. He tried vigorously to open the door. He was unable to turn the knob. As he shook the door it made more noise. The woman slowly turned her head toward Zeke. Here sewn shut eyes me him. She began to open them; the stitches that held them shut began to snap one by one. Even more blood started pouring out of her sockets. At the same time the half of her mouth that had been sewn shut began to rip open. Zeke fell to the floor. The woman's eyes were fully open staring directly into Zeke’s. They were a light blue, almost exactly like lilies. Her eyes began to quiver and tears started pouring out.

The woman looked at Zeke and said "Help Me"

She raised both her hands and crept toward Zeke. Zeke shielded his face with his arms and felt someone shaking them.

"You ok son?"

It was a man's voice. Zeke looked up to see the sheriff standing over him. The kitchen was back to what it was. The archway had been restored. And in the living room the wallpaper was back up as. Zeke stood up and ran  into the living room and began ripping down the wall paper.

The Sherriff said "Hey what are you doing Zeke? What's the matter?"

Zeke pulled away all of the wall paper but all that was behind it was the wall, no blood and no hidden message.

Zeke began to cry, "I don't get it, what the fuck is happening?"

The Sherriff looked at him and asked "What are you talking about?"

Zeke began to rant "The chair with the man over there" He pointed to the corner, “the Woman in the kitchen, what happened to her?”

"I don't know what you are going on about; do you need me to take you to the hospital?"

"Lillian!" Zeke bolted out of the house and into his car straight to the hospital. He was relieved to see that she was ok. He told her what had just happened. He decided to spend the night at the hospital with Lillian. He had barely closed his eyes when he woke back up in the hospital room. It was different though. The walls looked brand new and the hospital bed was metal instead of plastic. In the bed was the woman from the kitchen. Her face was now severely burnt. Her eyes were melted shut. Her lips were gone and mouth was sealed. Zeke was less afraid this time of the woman. Through the window he could see the man who was sitting in the green chair being arrested by a Sherriff.  He had a smirk painted on his face. He could hear the sheriff muffled through the door.

"James Carver, you have the right to remain silent. Anything you say can, and will, be used against you in court of law. You have the right to an attorney. If you cannot afford one, one will be appointed to you,"

Zeke's eyes widened, he looked back at the woman lying in the hospital bed. He walked over to the clip board hanging at the foot. Her name was smeared off, illegible.  He looked back at the woman who was weeping. The door to the hospital room began to open. A nurse stepped through and asked Zeke "Evening, just here to check up on Lillian, you must be Mr. Lawson?"

Zeke looked back at the bed and saw his wife there once again, asleep.

"Yes I'm Mr. Lawson" Zeke absentmindedly replied.

The nurse tended to Lillian checked some machines, and left. Lillian was still fast asleep. As soon as she woke up they left the town for good, never looking back.
