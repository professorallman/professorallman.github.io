Austin Allman

Dream

I stepped out of the backdoor of my house looking back at the same mauve coloring of the walls I never quite understood. The sun was beating down on everything around me and yet I felt no heat. I noticed that my good friend Dave had walked out with me. It was odd that he was there since his parents normally did not let him come over.  I closed the door behind us and turn around. It was my fenced in yard, and right over the fence was my neighbor’s house. I always thought how his siding was so cool. He never had to paint his walls; the rain would keep them clean. Our house was less fortunate and had a spackled sheetrock mixture that made the walls feel bumpy when you moved your hand across it. We would have to paint it every couple of year, and painting time always stuck out in my mind since I was terrified of heights and yet was coerced into going up a latter somehow. I turned to Dave and asked him what he was doing over.

"We were going to play with Steve remember? He’s already in back waiting for us"

"Oh right" I replied, though I didn’t remember this at all. Nevertheless I was still excited to see Steve, he was the type of best friend every 9 year old should have. We made our way across the yard when a sharp pain hit me in the stomach. It was like my big brother Kyle had just punched me in the gut. I wrapped my arms around my stomach and hunched over.

"You ok Drew?"

"Yeah I’ll be ok." I unbent myself but the pain was still there. When I was upright again I could see Steve and his sister Jill just to the right of the garage. The garage shared the same mauve color as the house. It was built fifty feet away from the house with the yard extending alongside it. The yard continued past the garage for another ten feet and was enclosed with a double wide fence door that opened outward toward the garage door. Dave and I both waved at them and they both smiled and waved back. We started walking toward them when Jill let out a shriek. Jill fell to the ground staring out of the back gate doors. I couldn’t see what had her terrified. I turned to look at Dave, but he was already gone from my side running back to the house. I turned back to see a man standing over Jill. I started to run as fast as I could toward Jill but only managed to take a step as the man slowly crouched down in front of Jill. He was wearing blue jeans covered in mud tucked into large black work boots. His skin was peeling from his arms; I was still far away but could smell blood and rotten garbage coming from him. He had on a dirtied white shirt that was torn as if it was clawed by a wild animal. The man extended his decaying hand out and grabbed Jill by the neck. As he did this his mouth began to form a smile. His jaw lowered but his cheeks could not handle the weight and holes began to form on the side of his mouth exposing his teeth, sharp as daggers.  Blood began to gush out of the newly formed holes in the man’s face, covering his clothing with red streams. His free hand began to move behind him, there was a metal red wagon I did not notice before. It was overflowing with blood with a head sticking out, but someone had removed the eyes. I looked behind the wagon; there was a trail of body parts on the ground, including the eyes that belonged to the head. I looked back at the wagon, it was Steve! From the pool of blood the man pulled out a large machete. He raised it high and swung it across Jill’s midsection. Jill’s eyes rolled back as her bottom half separated from the top. Still clutching her throat the man threw the top half of Jill behind himself. Her body spun in circles through the air as guts began to fly out in every direction. She landed neatly next to her brother, facing him. The man now turned toward me. I was still trying to run toward him but i couldn't lift my legs. As he fixed his gaze on me my whole body began to creep toward him. I dropped myself to the ground and helplessly clenched the earth. As I continued to gravitate toward the man my fingernails ripped out, filling the trenches my fingers had formed with blood.

***

I stepped out of the backdoor of my house looking back at the sky blue coloring of the walls. I noticed that my good friend Dave had walked out with me.  I closed the door behind us and began to turn around. It was my fenced in yard, and right over the fence was my neighbor’s brick house. I turned to Dave and asked him what he was doing over.

"We were going to play with Steve remember? He’s already in back waiting for us"

"Oh right" I replied. We made our way across the yard when a sharp pain hit me in the stomach. It was like my big sister Kylee had just punched me in the gut. I wrapped my arms around my stomach and hunched over.

"You ok Drew?"

"Yeah I’ll be ok." I unbent myself but the pain was still there. When I was upright again I could see Steve and his sister Jill just to the right of my garage. Dave and I both waved at them and they both smiled and waved back. We started walking toward them when Jill let out a shriek while falling to the ground and staring out of the back gate doors. I could see a man walking through the gate. I turned to look at Dave, our eyes met and we both turn and started running to the house. We bust through the door falling to the ground. We look back and see the man standing over Jill.

"You have to go help them!" Dave said panting.

I nodded and started running as fast as I could toward Jill. The man extended his hand out and grabbed Jill by the neck. I reached him and began to pull on his arm. His grip on Jill would not be subsided so easily. He swung his other hand around toward my temple. All I could see was the metal face of a hammer barreling toward me. It sounded like someone broke a pane of glass on my forehead, though instead of glass shards I saw bits of my skull flying toward the man. I was descending to the ground as the hammer recoiled from its strike with globs of my brain hanging from it. There was a beautiful rainbow of blood following me from where I had just been.

***

I stepped out of the backdoor of my house looking back at the hunter green coloring of the walls. I noticed that my good friend Dave had walked out with me. I closed the door behind us and began to turn around. This isn’t right. I set off running back toward the garage. I reach Steve and Jill.

"What’s the big rush Drew?" Steve asks me.

Before I could respond I fall to the ground, pain stabbing me in the stomach. I roll over and the man is standing over me. He smiles. He thrusts his large boots into my chest, bends over and draws a blade across my throat.

***

I stepped out of the backdoor of my house looking back at the eggshell white coloring of the walls. I noticed that my good friend Dave had walked out with me. I look up at the sky, it was crimson. The clouds swirled in circles around themselves, forming a perfect circle in the middle exposing the stars.

I turn to Dave and say "I think I’m going to stay inside today."
