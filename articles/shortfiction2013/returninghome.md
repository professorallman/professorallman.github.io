Returning Home

I can easily say this now, but I was over privilege spoiled brat growing up.  When I turned 18 I left my home on 456 Burgess Street for greener pastures. I did not tell my parents I was leaving. All they found was a note from me saying that I was safe and don’t bother looking for me.  It was two years ago that I had parted on my great adventure to only find out that the world was not what I thought it was. But that is a story for another time. Now I am home in Minnesota and ready to face my parents.

I arrived today at noon at the greyhound station on University Avenue in Saint Paul. Even though it had only been two years everything was changed. I have to cross over some tracks in the road that were never there. It looks like it is still under construction. Not many people are outside I guess this everyone is at work today. I continue up Rice for two blocks and take a left on Como. This area was still the same at least. I can see the Asian farmers market and the used car lot. Though even this street now has bike lanes on either side. I take a right onto front, almost home. Burgess Street!

Everything is gone…..

There are no houses. My block had been replaced by a massive yard full of dirt and machinery. I now have to find where my parents are.
