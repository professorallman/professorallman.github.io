Fisherman

It was a painting from Yahweh as light danced across the sky in the night. Each stroke of the brush was closely followed by a bellowing boom from the heavens. To not be outdone by God, Poseidon raised his mighty fist sending forth a blue furry throughout his domain. Assaulted by the heavens and the sea the SS Minnow remained as steadfast as her crew. Hand over hand to the symphony of the night the men pulled up their catch. Every man upon his flesh dawned gifts from Poseidon and in their soul an emptied chalice from God. Every man except Jim who had but a small kiss on the cheek from Poseidon, and at half Yahweh was full. Like a novice stepping on their dance partners toes Jim tried to work like the rest.

It was a stormy night as the crew worked vigorously to bring up the last catch of the day. Most of them were seasoned crab fisherman but Jim had just traveled up from Iowa. He was hoping to produce for his pregnant wife and two year old.He longed to speak to his wife but everytime the ship was in range of the mainland his wife would be at work. I was hard for Ji, to keep track of the days or what time it was since aboard the SS minnow the work day was not consistant. He searched out the captain and found him walking around the deck.

“Do you have the time?” Jim asked the captain

“It’s about half past three” he replied

“Im not from around here and need to palce a call back home, do you know the time in central time?”

The captain gave Jim an irritated look as if this was th e most idiotic question anyone had asked in his life. In a short voice he replied “Half past four”

Jim could sence he had irritated the captain so he attempted to make small talk with him. “interesting wheather is it not?”

“yeah it’s a bit cold” the captain said in his irritated tone.

Jim finally got the hint and said “ok thenm see you later”

Jim began to panic. He feared he may have upset the captain enough to be left on shore next time they made port. He hurredly returned back to work and began calculating how many more nets they had yet to pull up. His heart sank as he realized once again they would not make it back in time for him to talk to his wife.
